When Compare

Comparing bike insurance policies doesn’t begin and end with two-wheeler insurance premium comparison. In between, there are quite a few factors that you should base an online comparison on.

Here’s a checklist of that:

  • Shopping for Cheap Premiums? Know your cover first

    When you compare Two Wheeler Insurance, be sure of the type of cover you need. While The Motor Vehicles Act warrants that every two-wheeler have the basic Third Party Liability Only Policy, in order to ply on Indian roads, a more comprehensive Two Wheeler Insurance Policy provides coverage to your two-wheeler from regular losses/damages (as a result of man’s actions or otherwise).

    If you have your eyes trained only on low premium costs, you might have to settle with inappropriate or inadequate coverage. That’s the reason why you should ideally carry out a through comparison of the products and their specific features (including add-on covers) before finally moving ahead with one.

  • Consider Add-ons

    Add-ons take your Two Wheeler Insurance Policy past the basic plan mark. However, amidst a slew of add-on benefits, know which ones might not add much value to your policy. Additional covers such as Roadside Assistance Cover, Zero Depreciation Cover, or the Engine Protection Cover for that matter, might be more beneficial than others.

    Having said that, your insurance objectives, budget and driving habits should influence the add-on covers you opt for.

  • Be Familiar with the Insurer’s Claim Settlement History

    Being savvy with your insurer’s history of claim settlement would give you a valuable insight into its efficiency when it comes to getting a claim approved. Look up reviews and grievances posted by customers on the insurer’s web portal. Moreover, you can also probe into their after-sales and customer services for absolute peace of mind.

  • Check for Deductibles

    A compulsory deductible is a sum that you would have to pay from your own pocket at the time of a claim. A voluntary deductible, on the other hand, is an amount that you’ve consented to pay prior to claim settlement.

    Should you choose a higher deductible value, you’d be charged a relatively low premium on your Two Wheeler Insurance Policy. However, this will certainly affect the total claim amount (from the insurer) that you’re eligible for.

    Don’t skimp on factoring in these deductibles when you compare bike insurance policies.

  • Know all Inclusions and Exclusions

    As the maxim goes, the devil lies in the details. And your Two Wheeler Insurance Policy isn’t an exception to the norm. Read up the policy wording carefully as that would tell you exactly what’s included and what’s not. This way, you’d know whether the policy is worth your money.
